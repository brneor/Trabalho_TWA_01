What is a mechanical keyboard?

On any keyboard, the user taps a letter that actuates, or register, and tells the device to send a signal to the computer. When the key is on for a fraction of second, it sends a single letter to the computer; when it's on for longer, it sends the letter over and over.

On membrane keyboards, the key passes through a plastic layer with electrical contacts and then though a hone to a second layer that interrupts a circuit

Why mechanical?

Mechanical keyboards are not new in the world of computer peripherals.
